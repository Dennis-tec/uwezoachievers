ACCES Scholarship Program for College & University Students in Kenya

ACCES Scholarship Program is a non-profit agency that supports bright and needy Kenyan students by providing post-secondary Kenya scholarships. The scholarships are for study at Kenyan universities and technical colleges.

Requirements

To be eligible for consideration, the applicant must meet the following minimum criteria:

  1. Kenyan Citizen, below 30 years of age, and from Busia, Bungoma, Kakamega, and Vihiga Counties
  2. Minimum KCSE grade of C plain for college and C+ for University
  3. Those already attending College/University and who meet the above grades must maintain a minimum grade of B and above in academic performance
  4. Must be either enrolled or have an admission letter to a Kenyan learning institution accredited with the KNEC or KUCCPS. Post-Graduate Program Students are not eligible
  5. Students on Privately Sponsored Students’ Programmes (PSSP) in colleges and universities are not eligible to apply
  6. College/University students must not be fully funded by the Higher Education Loans Board (HELB). For those under the Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) Program, only the college/university undergraduates with a HELB loan of KShs.40,000 and below and demonstrating further financial need may be considered. ACCES Staff will verify the amount the candidate claims to have received from HELB
  7. Students with DISABILITY who did KCSE from Bungoma, Busia, Kakamega, Kisumu, Nandi, Siaya, TransNzoia, Uasin Gishu, and Vihiga counties are eligible to apply
  8. Must demonstrate the need for financial assistance by providing supporting documents outlining family circumstances. Recommendation letters alone, without other supporting documents, will not be sufficient

Process

  1. Download and fill out an information form, from ACCES Kenyato see if you will qualify for a scholarship.
  2. If you qualify, download and fill out the application formto apply.
  3. Applicants should provide a copy of birth certificate, national ID if applicable, high school leaving certificate, and KCSE results slip.
  4. Note that a new form is issued for each intake.

Successful applicants will receive a partial college or university scholarship from ACCES.

Application Deadline

Applications are currently closed. Interested students are invited to apply a month after the release of KCSE results.

WMF Empowerment Through Education Scholarships in Kenya

The ETE program provides undergraduate scholarships to developing country nationals to study in their home country or a neighboring country.

Requirements

WMF’s ideal candidate is a student, male or female, from a country in the developing world, who:

  1. Completed secondary education, with good to excellent grades
  2. Will be studying in their country or another country in the developing world
  3. Plans to live and work in their own country after they graduate
  4. Has volunteered before applying for this scholarship and/or is willing to volunteer while receiving the WMF scholarship
  5. May have some other funds available for their education, but will not be able to go to school without a scholarship

Process

  1. Apply via the online scholarship application portal(preferred method) or via postal mail to the Foundation’s office.
  2. Record all of your service activities (dates, activities, time spent, photographs, etc.).
  3. Collect your secondary school transcript, national exam results and grading keys to interpret your results.
  4. Request two letters of recommendation on official stationery from two people who know you professionally/personally and can showcase the reasons you deserve this scholarship.
  5. Have all of your documents translated into English.
  6. Selected scholars will be emailed on August 1st. Their names will also be published on the WMI website homepage and Facebook page.

Application Deadline

Applications are currently closed. Applications open from December 1st to March 1st every year. Inclusive Disability Employment Fellows Program for East Africans.

Kenya School of Law Scholarships

The Law School Scholarships was launched by The Law Society of Kenya (LSK) Advocates' Benevolent Association to support two needy students enrolled in the Kenya School of Law every year.

Requirements

  1. Must be above 18 years of age, but not older than 27 years
  2. Must be a Kenyan national
  3. Must have a valid admission letter into the Advocates Training Programme or Paralegal Studies Programme from Kenya School of Law
  4. Be able to demonstrate financial needs

Process

  1. Duly completed Application form.
  2. Must submit an application in the prescribed format accompanied by:

    • Two colored passport-size photos

    • Certified copy of the birth certificate

    • Certified copy of national identification card

    • Certified copies of LLB academic transcripts and certificates

    • Evidence of previous financial support e.g. HELB; x. Certificate of Good Conduct

    • Certified copy of KCSE or equivalent

    • For orphans, certified copies of the death certificates of parents/guardians

    • A letter from your area Chief in support of your application

    • Plea statement on why you should be awarded the scholarship, clearly indicate your financial situation and how you have financed yourself so far

    • Any other supporting documents are accepted

Application Deadline

Applications are currently closed. Applications for students for the Advocates Training Programme should be received ten working days before the commencement of registration during the academic year the student intends to join.


Applications for students for the Diploma in Law (Paralegal Studies) should be received ten working days before the commencement of registration during the academic year the student intends to join. No applications shall be received after the lapse of the application period.

Kenya TIVET Bursary Scheme

The TEVET Bursary, under the Ministry of Higher Education (MOHE) is offered to vulnerable students who are not able to pay user fees for them to access skills training.

Eligibility

Kenyan students pursuing Certificate, Diploma, and Higher National Diploma courses in select Public Universities, select University Colleges, Public National Polytechnics, and approved TVET Colleges under the Ministry of Education are eligible for this loan and bursary.

Application Requirements

  1. A copy of the applicant’s admission letter from the institution
  2. A copy of the applicant’s National Identification Card
  3. Applicant’s KCSE result slip or certificate
  4. Copies of the parents’ National ID Cards or death certificates where the parent is deceased
  5. Copies of both guarantors’ National ID Card
  6. A copy of the applicant’s Bank ATM/Bankcard
  7. One recent colored passport size photograph of the applicant

Process

  1. Visit theHelb, to access the Student Portal and click on “create an account” to register.
  2. Once the account is created, log in and select the appropriate TVET First Time Loan Application Form [TLAF].
  3. Read the literature or view the videos on Financial Literacy and participate in the brief exercise online.
  4. Access and fill the loan application form.
  5. Print TWO copies of the duly filled TVET Loan Application Form [Mandatory].
  6. Have the forms appropriately filled, signed, and stamped by the relevant authorities.
  7. Attach all the documents as per the checklist and present one copy of the duly filled Loan Application Form at:

    (i) Any of the following Huduma centers nearest to you: Bomet, Bungoma, Chuka, Eldoret, Embu, Garissa, Nairobi GPO, Kakamega, Kericho, Kilifi, Kisii, Kisumu, Kitale, Kitui, Lodwar, Machakos, Makueni, Meru, Migori, Mombasa, Murang’a, Nakuru, Nandi Narok, Nyeri, Taita Taveta, Thika and West Pokot for free and secure delivery; or

    (ii)HELB Student Service Centre on Mezzanine 1, Anniversary Towers, University Way, Nairobi.

Application Deadline

Applications are currently open until January 1st, 2022.

Toyota Foundation Scholarships

The Toyota Kenya Foundation offers scholarships every year to students who are less privileged to study in a technical college or polytechnic in Kenya. As one of the diploma scholarships in Kenya, the foundation offers this financial award to only orphans and vulnerable children.

Eligibility

  1. Kenyan students from disadvantaged families
  2. Applicants must have gone through basic education and high school but are not able to raise tuition fees for higher education
  3. Applicants admitted to college education in the fields of medicine, engineering, environmental management & agriculture
  4. A minimum grade of C+ in KCSE and in their second year of study

Process

Students with the requisite qualifications should send copies of their birth certificate, national ID, KCSE certificate, and a handwritten cover letter to their college principal by 30th September.

Successful applicants will be contacted.

Application Deadline

The application is currently open until 30th September.

Wangari Maathai Scholarships for Kenya Women

This is an environmental innovations fund set up in memory of Prof. Wangari Maathai aimed at developing action-oriented young people with strong values and commitment to the conservation of the physical and social environment in Kenya.

Requirements

  1. Young women between the ages of 18 – 25 years who have demonstrated passion and personal commitment to environmental conservation
  2. Kenyan citizens currently residing in Kenya
  3. The scholarship is limited to applicants who have enrolled in a Kenyan tertiary institution
  4. Ability to demonstrate the environmental conservation project(s) they have started or have been involved in and how those initiatives have impacted people in their community, in ways that have improved environmental health and sustainability, and the fund’s goals of achieving a greener, cleaner, and a more sustainable community
  5. Individuals must also demonstrate their ability to successfully mobilize people and provide leadership in environmental advocacy

Process

  1. Detailed application guidelines and application forms can be downloaded using this link.
  2. Completed APPLICATION FORMS can be submitted by email to grants@kcdf.or.ke.
  3. Printed copies may be submitted to KCDF Offices in Pangani to be received before the mentioned deadline.
  4. Applicants must cross-check with the checklist that all the required attachments are included; otherwise incomplete applications will NOT be considered.

Application Deadline

Applications are currently closed. The scholarship fund is awarded annually.

Working to Advance African Women Scholarship

WAAW Foundation offers $500 annual scholarships for need-based undergraduate female African students admitted to a University, College, or institute of higher learning in Africa.

Requirements

  1. Female students of African origin, living and studying in Africa
  2. Currently enrolled in an undergraduate B.S.degree program
  3. Studying STEM-related courses in a University or college in Africa
  4. Demonstrable financial need
  5. Excellent Academic Record
  6. Proven leadership, volunteering, and community service
  7. Below the age of 32 years
  8. Only students in their first and second years are eligible to apply
  9. Students in their third year if they are studying a five-year course

Process

Your application should include:

  1. The application form obtained by emailing scholarship@waawfoundation.org and filling it out completely.
  2. Personal and Contact Information.
  3. Educational background and Family Information.
  4. Statement of need should describe why scholarship funds are needed and what the funds will be used for if received.
  5. Responses to essay questions that address career goals and how you expect the WAAW scholarship will assist in your education.
  6. Selected scholars will be emailed on August 1st. Their names will also be published on the WMI website homepage and Facebook page.
  7. Have your essay responses ready before you begin the application.

    (i) In 500 words or less, please write an essay on your future career goals and how you expect the WAAW foundation scholarship will assist in fulfilling those goals.

    (ii) In 300 words or less, please describe what you believe about female education in Africa and its impact on research, development, or advancement in African economies.

    (iii) In 500 words or less, what is your proudest achievement to date.

    (iv) Describe in ten or fewer sentences why you need a scholarship. Tell us about your need or personal/family/financial situation and how the funds from the WAAW scholarship will assist your education and/or career plans

  8. Two academic/professional references. Note recommendation letters and transcripts ARE NOT REQUIRED at the time of application. ONLY after a candidate has been shortlisted. However, you must provide the names of 2 references in your application.
  9. Shortlisted candidates will be required to send the following additional items to complete this process

    (a) 2 references; one MUST be written by a professor from your institution of study and the other from an academic supervisor/advisor or mentor to be emailed by your referee to us.

    (b) A copy of a current signed and sealed transcript from your University to be emailed to transcripts@waawfoundation.org.

    (c) A copy of the student’s School identity card must be scanned and emailed to us.

Application Deadline

Applications are currently open until 2nd November.

EABL Scholarships for Study in Kenya Public Universities

EABL Foundation is investing in bright and needy students who cannot afford university education by offering full scholarships in the following degree courses: 4 years Bachelor of Commerce course; 4 years Bachelor of Business Information Technology course; 5 year Bachelor of Engineering course and 4 year Bachelor of Food Science course.

  3. Applications to be accompanied by documentation showing financial neediness.

Application Deadline

Kenya Oshwal Aid HELB Scholarships

The scholarships fund 20 students for the total tenure of study for an undergraduate degree program for both tuition and partial upkeep at Kshs.100,000 per student annually. The fund seeks to support needy first-year students in Kenyan universities.

Oshwal Kenya Scholarships finances the following courses of study:

Business Studies and Management, Fine Arts, Political Science, Law, Architecture, Information Systems and Technology, Sociology, Environmental Studies, Agricultural, Economics, and Pharmacy

Eligibility

  1. Kenyan Citizen with a Residential Status in Kenya
  2. Have sat for the KCSE or GCE or equivalent level not more than one year before the application
  3. Have obtained a mean grade of B+ or equivalent and above, with at least B plain in English and Mathematics
  4. Demonstrate financial need
  5. Demonstrate leadership qualities, community service/spirit, concern for others, talent, concern about the environment, and a solid character
  6. Provide reference letters from two referees one of whom must be the secondary school headteacher
Note

The applicant must show proof of placement at a university before the scholarship is awarded.

The applicant is expected to provide academic progress reports and reference letters from the university yearly to maintain funding for the ongoing period of study.

The scholarship is paid directly by the funder to the student.

Process

  1. Applicants should access Visa Oshwal Scholarship Application Form [LAF] from the Helb.
  2. Print TWO copies of the duly filled Loan Application Form.
  3. Have the printed form appropriately filled, signed, and stamped by the relevant authorities and referees.
  4. Sign the form and attach all the necessary documents as indicated in the checklist appearing on the last page of the LAF.
  5. Present one copy of the LAF to HELB. Retain one copy of the duly filled LAF.

Application Deadline

Applications are currently open until January 1st, 2022.
