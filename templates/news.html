News & Updates

Updates

The following programs are currently accepting applications:

Toyota Foundation Scholarships

The Toyota Kenya Foundation offers scholarships every year to students who are less privileged to study in a technical college or polytechnic in Kenya. As one of the diploma scholarships in Kenya, the foundation offers this financial award to only orphans and vulnerable children. The application is currently open until 30th February.

Working to Advance African Women Scholarship

WAAW Foundation offers $500 annual scholarships for need-based undergraduate female African students admitted to a University, College, or institute of higher learning in Africa. Applications are currently open until 2nd March

Kenya Oshwal Aid HELB Scholarships

The scholarships fund 20 students for the total tenure of study for an undergraduate degree program for both tuition and partial upkeep at Kshs.100,000 per student annually. The fund seeks to support needy first-year students in Kenyan universities. Oshwal Kenya Scholarships finances the following courses of study: Business Studies and Management, Fine Arts, Political Science, Law, Architecture, Information Systems and Technology, Sociology, Environmental Studies, Agricultural, Economics, and Pharmacy. Applications are currently open until March 1st, 2024.

Kenya TIVET Bursary Scheme

The TEVET Bursary, under the Ministry of Higher Education (MOHE) is offered to vulnerable students who are not able to pay user fees for them to access skills training. Applications are currently open until Feb 15th, 2023.

History

Uwezo Achievers was founded by Dennis Yiaile, the President of this program, in 2021. Dennis is currently a student at Haverford College, USA, Kenya Scholar Access Program (KenSAP) alumnus 2020, and Equity Leadership Program(ELP) alumnus 2019. He hails from a humble background in Ngosuani, a rural village in southern Kenya. Like many children in this locality, he had limited knowledge about the outside world but that didn’t deter his ambition to study at a liberal art institution in North America.

To help gifted and needy scholars who are unable to advance their education dreams due to lack of tuition fees, he founded Uwezo Achievers. The program targets Kenya Certificate Of Secondary Education(KCSE) and Kenya Certificate Of Primary Education (KCPE) graduates from low-income backgrounds, particularly those from rural settings in Kenya. The program, voluntarily, exposes them to diverse scholarship programs that can cater to their education expenses depending on their level of studies.


“Wherever my pursuit of knowledge takes me, I hope that my story, endeavors, abilities, and experiences will motivate students from low-income backgrounds, precisely those from rural settings in Kenya to follow my path, or to chart their own, with a quality education.”Dennis Yiaile
