What We Do

Uwezo Achievers is a free, non-profit program that provides access to high school and undergraduate scholarships for under-resourced Kenyan primary and high school graduates who would otherwise have no chance to continue with their studies. After graduating from primary or high school, many students who performed well academically end up dropping out of school. Ninety percent of these students come from low-income backgrounds that can’t afford the cost of their education.

While this happens, there are diverse high school and undergraduate programs in Kenya that can cater to their studie's fees and expenses. Furthermore, free college programs that can help them access world-class undergraduate institutions abroad for free exist. To join these programs, students are required to go through a rigorous application process that interviews and personal essays. Unfortunately, many students, especially those from the rural parts of Kenya, are uninformed about such programs. Concurrently, most of them have limited knowledge about the application processes, specifically what to expect during interviews and how to approach essay questions. This has made gifted and needy students miss these opportunities hence terminating their studies and dreams.

To solve this, Uwezo Achievers introduces students from every part of Kenya to these programs depending on their level of education. Students can explore a variety of organizations that offer education scholarships in Kenya and programs that can help them get scholarships abroad. The application process, deadline, and requirements are available to scholars on our website. Further, we help students prepare for their interviews and guide them on essay writing.

Uwezo Achievers encourage students to use their education for the benefit of Kenyans less fortunate than themselves and to contribute to Kenya’s development in ways commensurate with the quality of their education.
